## 📋 Photography Style Guide

### What We Want

✓ **Authentic:** Real people, real moments, unscripted energy  
✓ **High contrast:** Vibrant colors, sharp shadows, clear depth  
✓ **Storytelling:** Show activities, faces, emotions, not just scenery  
✓ **Consistent tone:** Warm colors, slightly desaturated details, golden hour preferred  
✓ **Professional:** Sharp focus, good lighting, clean compositions  
✓ **Diverse perspectives:** Wide shots, detail shots, portraits, environmental  

### What We Don't Want

✗ Blurry or out-of-focus images  
✗ Overexposed skies (use ND filters if needed)  
✗ Unaware/non-consenting people in backgrounds  
✗ Watermarks or branding on image itself  
✗ Stock photo obviousness (clichéd poses, fake smiles)  
✗ Desaturated or dull color grades

## 🎬 Shooting Checklist

**Location Scouts:**
- [ ] Scout shots during golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
- [ ] Identify key vantage points
- [ ] Note lighting conditions at different times of day
- [ ] Check for distracting backgrounds

**Diversity:**
- [ ] Wide landscape shots (context)
- [ ] Detail/close-ups (texture, culture)
- [ ] People/portraits (connection, humanity)
- [ ] Activities/action shots (journey, adventure)

**Technical:**
- [ ] Shoot RAW + JPG
- [ ] Consistent white balance (set manually)
- [ ] Lens choices: 16-35mm for landscapes, 50-85mm for portraits
- [ ] Use tripod for stability in low light
- [ ] Take multiple angles per scene

**Cultural Sensitivity:**
- [ ] Always ask permission before photographing people
- [ ] Respect sacred sites and ceremonies
- [ ] Work with local guides on sensitive cultural moments
- [ ] Compensate subjects appropriately

## 🖥️ Post-Processing Standards

### Color Grading
- Warm tone: Add slight yellow/orange in shadows
- Slightly crushed blacks (richness without blown blacks)
- Vibrant but natural saturation (not oversaturated)
- Consistent preset across all images for series cohesion

### Sharpening
- Moderate sharpening for web (avoid over-sharp, clinical look)
- Lens correction for distortion
- Chromatic aberration removal if present

### Levels & Curves
- Target: Vibrant mid-tones, detail in shadows and highlights
- Avoid: Flat, washed-out, or overly contrasted look
- White balance: 5000-6500K (neutral-warm)

### Export Settings (Critical)
- **Format:** JPEG for web
- **Profile:** sRGB (web-safe color space)
- **Quality:** 85% (preserve detail, avoid compression artifacts)
- **Dimensions:** Full resolution or as specified by use case

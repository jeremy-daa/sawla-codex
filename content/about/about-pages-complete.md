# About Sawla Tours — Ethiopia's Boutique Travel Specialist

## Our Story

Sawla Tours was founded in Addis Ababa in 2009, operating initially as Ethio Renaissance Tours. The company grew from a simple premise: that international visitors to Ethiopia were receiving a fraction of what the country had to offer, not because the experiences weren't available, but because nobody had organised them in a way that matched Ethiopia's actual character.

Seventeen years later, Sawla Tours is one of Ethiopia's most established boutique operators. We are based in Addis Ababa, led by Ethiopians, and staffed almost entirely by Ethiopian nationals. This is not a branding decision — it reflects a conviction that the most important thing we can offer a visitor to Ethiopia is genuine insider knowledge, accumulated over lifetimes of living in this country.

## What Makes Sawla Different

Most tour operators show you Ethiopia. Sawla Tours helps you understand it.

The difference is in the approach. Before we build an itinerary, we have a conversation — about what you have already seen, what you are looking for, what kind of traveller you are. The answer shapes everything: where we go, how long we spend in each place, which guide we assign, which lodge we choose.

We also have something no other Ethiopian tour operator has: **Sawla Films** — an in-house documentary production division. Our team has filmed across all 12 regional states of Ethiopia. The footage archive is extraordinary. This capability means our guides don't just describe the wildlife, history, and culture — they document it. When you travel with Sawla Tours, you travel with the people who made the films about the places you are visiting.

## Our Philosophy

Ethiopia is not a backdrop. It is the foreground.

The country has 3,000 years of recorded history, 80+ languages, 16 distinct cultural regions, and a natural world found nowhere else on earth. The right journey through Ethiopia doesn't take you past these things — it places you inside them.

We are not a company that believes in checklists. We are not interested in rushing you through six UNESCO sites in five days. We are interested in the moment when a visitor from another country encounters something they had no framework to prepare for — a 12th-century liturgy in an excavated rock church at 4am, or an Ethiopian wolf hunting at dawn on a 4,000-metre plateau — and leaves changed.

That is why we do this.

# Why Travel With Sawla Tours

## We Are Ethiopian

Sawla Tours is not a foreign company operating in Ethiopia. We are an Ethiopian company, led by an Ethiopian team, with guides who are nationals of the regions they work in. This matters more than it might seem.

A guide from Lalibela explaining the theology of the rock churches is different from a guide who studied about them. A driver from the Omo Valley navigating the road to Turmi is different from one who learned the route from a map. The knowledge our team carries is not acquired — it is lived.

## We Work at a Different Depth

Our clients are typically not first-time Africa travellers. They are people who have been to Kenya or Tanzania, who have done the classic routes, and who are looking for something different. They come to Ethiopia because they want depth, not breadth. They want to understand, not just see.

Sawla Tours is designed for this kind of traveller. Our itineraries are structured around understanding — with guides who can answer difficult questions, with time built in for conversations with local people, with visits that go beyond the UNESCO placard.

## Sawla Films

The in-house documentary production capability of Sawla Films is not a marketing advantage. It is a genuine difference in how we experience and communicate Ethiopia.

When Sawla Films films the Ethiopian wolf on the Bale Mountains plateau, the footage is informed by hours of prior observation. When we film the gelada herds on the Simien escarpment, we know individual animals. This accumulated knowledge — of behaviour, of timing, of light — is what our wildlife guides carry into the field with you.

## Our Commitments

**To you:** We will respond to all enquiries within 24 hours. We will be honest about what is and is not possible. We will not oversell. We will tell you when a destination is not right for your travel style or timing.

**To Ethiopia:** We employ Ethiopian nationals at all levels. We use locally-owned accommodation wherever possible. We follow ethical protocols in all community and wildlife encounters. We are building a company that will still be operating in Ethiopia in another seventeen years.

# How We Work — The Sawla Tours Process

## Step 1: You Tell Us What Matters

A short conversation. Fifteen minutes on the phone or a few messages back and forth. We ask you about past trips, about what you enjoyed and what frustrated you, about the kind of traveller you are. We ask what you want to feel, not just what you want to see.

This conversation is the most important part of the process. Everything else — destinations, duration, accommodation, guiding style — follows from it.

## Step 2: We Design Your Journey

One of our specialists builds a bespoke itinerary. No templates. No group departure schedules. No "most popular" routes recommended because they are most popular.

The itinerary is a document: day by day, destination by destination, with the reasoning for each choice explained. Why this lodge, not that one. Why three nights in Bale, not two. Why the Tigray rock churches are worth the extra day.

## Step 3: You Refine, We Perfect

Two or three rounds of iteration. You ask questions. We adjust. You might want to cut a destination to slow the pace. You might want to add a festival. You might have dietary requirements we need to accommodate. Each iteration brings the journey closer to being specifically yours.

We have found that clients who engage with this process produce the best journeys. Your input is not a complication — it is the raw material.

## Step 4: You Arrive, We Are There

Our team meets you at Bole International Airport. From that moment, Ethiopia is handled. Your guide has the itinerary, the local contacts, the emergency protocols, and — more importantly — seventeen years of ground-level knowledge.

You focus on experiencing Ethiopia. We focus on everything else.

# Responsible Travel — Our Commitments

## Community First

Every Ethiopian journey creates an economic footprint. We are deliberate about where that footprint lands.

Sawla Tours uses locally-owned guesthouses and lodges wherever quality standards allow. We employ guides from the regions they work in. We use local food suppliers and — wherever possible — local service providers at every point in the journey.

In the Omo Valley, we operate according to protocols developed with community leaders: photography payments go directly to the individuals photographed, we do not visit communities without advance arrangement, and a portion of every Omo Valley journey fee is directed to the community health post and school fund in villages we visit regularly.

## Wildlife Ethics

We follow strict protocols in all wildlife encounters:
- Minimum approach distances maintained for all species
- Vehicles remain on established tracks in national parks
- No feeding, baiting, or artificially attracting wildlife
- No visits to breeding sites or dens during sensitive periods

We are financial supporters of the Ethiopian Wolf Conservation Programme (EWCP), which runs the vaccination campaign that has been the primary factor preventing disease-driven wolf population crashes.

## Environmental Practice

Mobile tented camps: solar-powered lighting, composting toilets, strict leave-no-trace protocols.

All Sawla Tours ground transport: carbon offset through verified programmes.

Single-use plastic: eliminated from all Sawla Tours operations since 2022.

The Addis Ababa Masterclass is not a fixed bus loop. It is a private half-day or full-day program built around history, food, coffee, art, faith, politics, contemporary life, business travel or a long layover.

Sawla uses conservative airport buffers and realistic cross-city timing. A six-hour connection does not equal six hours of touring, and distant destinations are not marketed as same-day road excursions.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long should my layover be?

A practical airside-to-city program often needs at least 8–10 hours, depending on visa, baggage, traffic and airline check-in. Sawla reviews the exact flights.

### Can I visit Lalibela on a day trip from Addis?

A same-day flight may occasionally be technically possible, but it is fragile and too rushed for a premium experience. Sawla recommends at least two nights.

### What happens if my inbound flight is delayed?

The guide shortens or changes the route according to the remaining safe time. Airport return takes priority.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

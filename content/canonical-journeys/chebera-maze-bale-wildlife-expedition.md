## Elephants, endemic antelope and Afroalpine predators across three very different reserves

This expedition links southwest forest-savanna, Maze’s open grasslands and Bale’s Afroalpine plateau. The target story is Ethiopia’s ecological range: elephant and buffalo country, Swayne’s hartebeest habitat, mountain nyala, Ethiopian wolves and forest species.

It is not a conventional lodge safari. Long roads, simple park accommodation or mobile camp and limited communications are part of the experience, and sightings remain opportunistic.


## Why this journey works

- Targets species and conservation landscapes rarely combined elsewhere
- Uses mobile field logistics rather than pretending infrastructure is uniform

## Frequently asked questions

### Is this similar to a Kenya or Tanzania safari?

No. Wildlife densities and infrastructure are different. The strength is rare species, remote habitats and conservation context.

### What accommodation is used?

The final proposal may combine town hotels, park rooms and mobile camp. Each property and field setup is described honestly before booking.

### Are elephants guaranteed in Chebera?

No. The park is important elephant habitat, but distribution, weather, roads and ranger information affect encounters.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

## From Addis coffee culture to Jimma history and Kafa forest origins

This journey follows coffee as plant, ecosystem, livelihood, trade and daily Ethiopian ritual. Addis introduces contemporary roasting and café culture; Jimma adds historical and commercial context; Kafa brings forest ecology, farms and local knowledge.

Harvest activity varies by altitude and year, so the product is designed around specific origins rather than one national date. Outside harvest, farm management, forest ecology, processing infrastructure and professional tasting remain valuable.

## Frequently asked questions

### When is the Ethiopian coffee harvest?

Harvest broadly runs from around October into January or February, but timing varies by region, altitude, variety and rainfall.

### Can Sawla arrange professional cupping?

Yes, subject to appointment and host availability. Tell us whether the session is educational, commercial or technical.

### Can I visit outside harvest season?

Yes. Forest ecology, farm management, processing infrastructure and producer discussions remain valuable, but cherry picking cannot be promised.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

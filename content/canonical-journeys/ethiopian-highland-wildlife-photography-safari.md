## Ethiopian wolves, geladas and mountain landscapes with patient field time

This expedition focuses on Ethiopia’s strongest highland wildlife subjects: Ethiopian wolves on Bale’s Afroalpine plateau and geladas against the Simien escarpment. Mountain nyala, raptors and landscape work broaden the portfolio.

Multiple dawn and late-day sessions allow the field team to respond to weather and animal movement. No species is guaranteed, and ethical distance overrides a desired frame.


## Why this journey works

- Dedicated time for animals within their landscape
- Route can retain value if one park module changes

## Frequently asked questions

### Are Ethiopian wolves guaranteed?

No. Bale offers the best opportunity, but movement, disease, weather and park conditions affect every sighting.

### What lenses are most useful?

A flexible long lens such as 100–400mm or 200–600mm is useful, with a shorter zoom for animals in landscape and a fast body for low light.

### How much walking is required?

Most Bale work uses vehicle access with short walks. Simien photography can be adapted, but altitude and uneven ground remain important.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

## A highly conditional floodplain expedition built around current wildlife intelligence

Gambella’s floodplains are connected to one of Africa’s least-known large-mammal systems, including white-eared kob movements across the borderlands. That strategic value does not make the experience predictable.

The product is intentionally presented as an expedition concept rather than a routinely bookable safari. The route, field base and even whether kob concentrations are realistically accessible must be reassessed before every proposal.

## Day-by-day itinerary

### Day 1 — Arrive in Addis Ababa

Review the final feasibility assessment, equipment, flights, field base, medical and communications plan. Meet your Sawla representative, transfer privately and review the route, current operations, baggage, health considerations and the pace planned for the days ahead.

**Overnight:** Addis Ababa

### Day 2 — Fly to Gambella

Meet the local field team and reconfirm park, road, flood and wildlife information before leaving town. Observation distance and habitat protection take priority over a desired sighting, with alternative nature and landscape work retained for quiet periods.

**Overnight:** Gambella

### Day 3 — Move to the field base

Travel by the current authorized route, establish camp or use the best operating base and begin late-day observation. The specialist guide adjusts timing and habitat focus according to recent field intelligence rather than following a fixed game-drive circuit.

**Overnight:** Gambella field base

### Day 4 — Full floodplain wildlife day

Use local intelligence to search appropriate habitat for kob, other mammals and birds without promising a migration spectacle. Current ranger information, weather and animal movement shape the route, and no species, behavior or unobstructed photographic position is guaranteed.

**Overnight:** Gambella field base

### Day 5 — Second field day and return

Repeat or change habitat according to sightings and conditions, then return toward Gambella with a safety margin. Observation distance and habitat protection take priority over a desired sighting, with alternative nature and landscape work retained for quiet periods.

**Overnight:** Gambella

### Day 6 — Fly to Addis Ababa

Use the confirmed flight and retain a buffer before international departure. The final transfer is planned with a protected connection buffer; a further Addis Ababa night is recommended when domestic and international tickets are separate.

**Overnight:** Addis Ababa or departure


## Frequently asked questions

### Is the white-eared kob migration guaranteed?

No. Movement is seasonal, transboundary and affected by floodplain conditions. The expedition is offered only when current intelligence supports a realistic plan.

### Why is this called an expedition concept?

Because access, logistics and wildlife concentration are too variable for a continuously bookable product. Every proposal begins with new feasibility work.

### What happens if Gambella cannot operate?

Sawla proposes a pre-agreed alternative such as Chebera, Bale or another specialist route and applies the booking terms stated in the proposal.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

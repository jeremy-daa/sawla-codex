## Ethiopian Christmas among pilgrims and rock-hewn churches

Genna in Lalibela is a pilgrimage and worship experience before it is a festival product. Pilgrims gather around the rock-hewn churches, chanting and praying through the night before the Christmas liturgy.

The broader route through Lake Tana and Gondar establishes the history and visual language of Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity, while extra time in Lalibela protects against crowds and schedule changes.

## Frequently asked questions

### When is Ethiopian Christmas?

Genna is generally celebrated on January 7, with important worship beginning on the evening of January 6. The local program is confirmed annually.

### Can non-Orthodox visitors attend the worship?

Visitors can usually observe from permitted areas when dressed and behaving respectfully. Access can change according to clergy, crowd and conservation needs.

### Is this primarily a photography tour?

No. It is a pilgrimage-led cultural journey. Photography is possible, but worship and the dignity of pilgrims come first.

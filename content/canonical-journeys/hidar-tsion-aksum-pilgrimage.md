## Sacred Aksum, archaeology and one of Ethiopia’s major Marian pilgrimages

Hidar Tsion draws pilgrims to the St Mary of Zion precinct in Aksum, a place central to Ethiopian Orthodox identity and belief about the Ark of the Covenant. The journey treats the observance as living faith, not as proof of disputed historical claims.

Archaeological days before the pilgrimage create context through the stelae field, inscriptions, tombs and the Aksumite landscape. A Gheralta extension is included only when current access and the traveler’s mobility make it appropriate.

## Day-by-day itinerary

### Day 1 — Arrive Addis Ababa

Arrival, transfer, and briefing. Meet your Sawla representative, transfer privately and review the route, current operations, baggage, health considerations and the pace planned for the days ahead.

**Overnight:** Addis Ababa

### Day 2 — Fly to Axum

Fly north to the ancient Aksumite capital. Afternoon orientation. Travel is treated as part of the journey, with selected landscape or cultural stops rather than an unrealistic race between destinations.

**Overnight:** Aksum

### Day 3 — Axum

Full day among the stelae, the underground tombs, St Mary of Zion, and the Queen of Sheba ruins. Time is reserved for context, questions and less hurried viewpoints instead of compressing the site into a rapid monument stop.

**Overnight:** Aksum

### Day 4 — Drive to Gheralta

Journey into the Gheralta massif, the heart of Tigray's cliff churches. The exact departure, stops and arrival program are adjusted to the confirmed timetable, road conditions and the group’s energy.

**Overnight:** Gheralta

### Day 5 — Abuna Yemata Guh

The famous cliff climb to one of the most dramatic churches on earth, with its preserved frescos. The visit is paced for interpretation and quiet observation, with active worship and local access taking priority over a checklist.

**Overnight:** Gheralta

### Day 6 — Maryam Korkor and Daniel Korkor

Further cliff churches set high in the Gheralta, each reached on foot. Time is reserved for context, questions and less hurried viewpoints instead of compressing the site into a rapid monument stop.

**Overnight:** Gheralta

### Day 7 — Return to Aksum for pilgrimage preparation

Travel back to Aksum with time for rest, orientation, crowd guidance and the latest local program before Hidar Tsion. The guide explains the religious sequence and manages respectful positioning without obstructing worshippers, processions or official crowd controls.

**Overnight:** Aksum

### Day 8 — Hidar Tsion pilgrimage in Aksum

Observe the principal pilgrimage sequence from permitted areas with a local specialist. Worship, safety and local direction take priority over access or photography.

**Overnight:** Aksum

### Day 9 — Fly to Addis and Depart

Morning flight to Addis and international connection. The final transfer is planned with a protected connection buffer; a further Addis Ababa night is recommended when domestic and international tickets are separate.

**Overnight:** Departure or Addis Ababa


## Frequently asked questions

### Can visitors see the Ark of the Covenant?

No. The chapel associated with the Ark tradition is not open to visitors. Sawla presents the belief respectfully without claiming archaeological proof.

### When is Hidar Tsion?

It is observed according to the Ethiopian calendar, usually in late November or early December. The exact annual dates and local program are reconfirmed.

### Is Gheralta guaranteed?

No. It is an optional module dependent on regional access, roads, local services and the traveler’s fitness.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

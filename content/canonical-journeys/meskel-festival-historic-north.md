## The Demera bonfire in Addis followed by Lake Tana, Gondar and Lalibela

Meskel commemorates the finding of the True Cross and is recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage. In Addis Ababa, the Demera bonfire gathers clergy, choirs and large crowds in Meskel Square.

The route then moves to Lake Tana’s church art, Gondar’s royal period and Lalibela’s rock-hewn sanctuaries so the event sits within a wider northern Christian narrative.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the difference between Demera and Meskel?

The public bonfire ceremony is commonly called Demera and takes place on the eve of the feast. Meskel is the religious commemoration of the finding of the True Cross.

### Will the tour always be nine days?

Nine days is the core sample, but a tenth may be recommended when northern flight timings make Lalibela too compressed.

### Can Meskel be photographed?

Yes from permitted areas, but crowd safety and worship take priority. Flash, drones and intrusive close-ups are not assumed.

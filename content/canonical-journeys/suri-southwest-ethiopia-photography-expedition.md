## Four Kibish field days for consent-led portraiture, cattle culture and landscape

The expedition is built around four full field days near Kibish rather than a rushed arrival, one portrait session and departure. That time allows local introductions, repeat access and work that includes landscape, cattle, daily life and individual voice.

Consent is negotiated person by person and can be withdrawn. A local coordinator shapes the day, while the expedition team manages camp, vehicles, power, water and image backup. Donga or other ceremonies are never promised.


## Why this journey works

- Four field days create better work than a compressed Suri stop
- Remote logistics and consent are treated as production requirements
- The route leaves contingency time for roads and local decisions

## Highlights

- Southwest coffee and forest transition
- Mobile camp or best current Kibish base
- Repeat permission-led portrait sessions
- Cattle and landscape storytelling
- Market or Donga only when genuinely occurring
- Charging, backup and dust-management systems

## Day-by-day itinerary

### Day 1 — Arrive in Addis Ababa

Meet the photography and logistics team for equipment, consent, route, medical and communications briefing. Meet your Sawla representative, transfer privately and review the route, current operations, baggage, health considerations and the pace planned for the days ahead.

**Overnight:** Addis Ababa

### Day 2 — Travel to Jimma

Fly or drive southwest and use the afternoon for coffee-country context and equipment checks. The field team protects time for patient observation, ethical positioning, charging and backups rather than promising a staged photographic outcome.

**Overnight:** Jimma

### Day 3 — Jimma to Mizan Teferi

Continue through forested landscapes, meet additional field staff and finalize supplies. Vehicle position, walking distance and the order of locations are adapted to weather, current activity and the agreed photographic brief.

**Overnight:** Mizan Teferi

### Day 4 — Mizan to Kibish

Travel by 4×4 on the remote road, meet the local coordinator and establish the camp or current base. Light, consent, subject availability and repeat access guide the session; the day retains an alternative visual plan if conditions change.

**Overnight:** Kibish area

### Day 5 — Suri field day one

Begin with introductions and landscape. Photograph only after the local process and individual permission are clear. The field team protects time for patient observation, ethical positioning, charging and backups rather than promising a staged photographic outcome.

**Overnight:** Kibish area

### Day 6 — Suri field day two

Work around cattle, homes, farming or other daily activity selected with hosts; avoid turning ordinary life into a performance. Vehicle position, walking distance and the order of locations are adapted to weather, current activity and the agreed photographic brief.

**Overnight:** Kibish area

### Day 7 — Suri field day three

Use repeat access, market information or a genuine ceremony if locally approved. Maintain an alternative visual plan. Light, consent, subject availability and repeat access guide the session; the day retains an alternative visual plan if conditions change.

**Overnight:** Kibish area

### Day 8 — Suri field day four

Return to agreed subjects or focus on wider environmental and social context, then back up and prepare for departure. Return using the confirmed schedule, allowing contingency time for roads, flights and baggage before any onward international departure.

**Overnight:** Kibish area

### Day 9 — Return toward Mizan or Jimma

Drive out with a full road and vehicle contingency margin. Vehicle position, walking distance and the order of locations are adapted to weather, current activity and the agreed photographic brief.

**Overnight:** Mizan Teferi or Jimma

### Day 10 — Return to Addis Ababa

Complete the domestic or road connection, protect the international-flight buffer and transfer or overnight. The final transfer is planned with a protected connection buffer; a further Addis Ababa night is recommended when domestic and international tickets are separate.

**Overnight:** Addis Ababa or departure


## Frequently asked questions

### How many full photography days are in Kibish?

Four. This is the core of the product and should not be compressed without changing the purpose of the expedition.

### Is Donga included?

Only when genuinely occurring, locally approved and safe. The expedition remains worthwhile without it.

### Can Sawla arrange releases for commercial work?

For commissioned or commercial use, the traveler or commissioning organization must provide legally appropriate forms and intended-use information. Sawla Tours can coordinate local explanation and consent logistics within the journey but does not provide legal advice or production-fixing services.

### Is this a fixed departure or a private journey?

It is a private sample journey rather than a scheduled group departure. Sawla adjusts the dates, sequence, accommodation, pace, guide profile and activity level around the traveler and current operating conditions.

### What is included in the final quotation?

The final proposal states every inclusion precisely. It normally covers agreed accommodation, private transport, guiding, listed visits and operational support; international flights, insurance, visas, personal spending and tips are normally separate unless stated otherwise.

### Can this itinerary be customized?

Yes. Extra nights, slower pacing, accommodation changes, specialist guides, domestic-flight alternatives and regional extensions can be built into the final journey.

## Procession, vigil and baptismal celebration in Ethiopia’s royal city

Timket commemorates the baptism of Christ and is observed across Ethiopia. Gondar is renowned for processions of tabots to Fasilides’ Bath, the overnight vigil and the dawn blessing of water.

The journey is designed around the full sequence rather than one colourful hour. Religious observance takes priority over photography and tourist positioning, and the Simien excursion provides landscape context before the intense festival days.

## Frequently asked questions

### When is Timket celebrated?

Timket is generally observed on January 19, with Ketera processions on the preceding day; calendar variations and the exact local program are reconfirmed annually.

### Is the water ceremony guaranteed to be visible?

The event occurs, but sightlines and access depend on crowd controls, official zones and arrival time. Sawla never promises an unobstructed position.

### Is Timket suitable for children?

Older children who can manage crowds and early starts may participate, but the route should be adjusted and a quieter viewing plan considered.

# Footsteps of History — Ethiopia's Heritage Tour
## 8 Days · Northern Circuit · Lalibela · Gondar · Simien

**Difficulty:** Easy–moderate  
**Best time:** October–June  
**Group size:** Private (1–8 people)  
**Price from:** USD 2,800 per person (based on 2 people sharing)

### Overview

Eight days through the heart of Ethiopia's ancient north. This is the essential northern circuit — the route that visitors return from with the clearest understanding that Ethiopia is unlike anywhere else in the world. Rock churches in Lalibela. Medieval castles in Gondar. Gelada baboons on the Simien escarpment. These are not Ethiopia's "highlights" — they are its foundations.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 — Arrival, Addis Ababa**  
Arrival at Bole International Airport. Met by your Sawla Tours driver and transferred to your hotel. Depending on arrival time, optional afternoon visit to the Entoto hills above the city for panoramic views, or the National Museum to meet Lucy. Welcome dinner at one of Addis's outstanding restaurants. Overnight Addis Ababa.

**Day 2 — Addis Ababa to Lalibela (fly)**  
Morning flight to Lalibela (approx 1 hour). Met at the airport, settled into your hotel. Afternoon orientation walk through Lalibela village. First visit to the Northern church cluster — Bet Medhane Alem, Bet Maryam, Bet Golgotha. These are among the finest in Lalibela; see them first in afternoon light. Evening briefing from your guide on tomorrow's deeper exploration. Overnight Lalibela.

**Day 3 — Lalibela churches (full day)**  
Early start. The Eastern church cluster — Bet Gabriel-Rufa'el (carved free-standing), Bet Merkorios, Bet Amanuel (the finest interior of all), Bet Abba Libanos. Then Bet Giyorgis: the most photographed church in Ethiopia, standing free in its excavated courtyard in the shape of a perfect cross. Afternoon: Asheton Maryam monastery — a 45-minute walk or mule ride above the town, sweeping views of the highland landscape. Overnight Lalibela.

**Day 4 — Lalibela to Gondar (fly)**  
Morning flight to Gondar. Afternoon visit to the Royal Enclosure — six castles in a walled compound, the most remarkable historical complex in sub-Saharan Africa. Fasilides Castle. Iyasu's Palace. The banqueting hall. Debre Berhan Selassie church: the ceiling painted entirely with 80 individual angel faces. Overnight Gondar.

**Day 5 — Gondar to Simien Mountains**  
Morning drive to the Simien Mountains National Park (approximately 3 hours). Afternoon walk on the escarpment — first encounter with the gelada monkeys. The escarpment viewpoint at Sankaber: 1,500 metres of cliff dropping to the valley floor. Overnight Simien Mountains.

**Day 6 — Simien Mountains — Chenek**  
Drive to Chenek (3,600m). This is the prime location for Walia ibex and the most reliable gelada viewing in the park. Walk along the cliff edge, watching the herds below. With luck, Ethiopian wolves are visible in the northern sections. Overnight Chenek or return to Gondar area lodge.

**Day 7 — Return to Gondar, fly to Addis**  
Morning in the Simien if staying overnight, then drive to Gondar airport for afternoon flight to Addis. Farewell dinner. Overnight Addis Ababa.

**Day 8 — Departure**  
Transfer to Bole International Airport for departure.

# Ethiopian Splendors — Classic Northern Circuit
## 11 Days · Lalibela · Axum · Gondar · Tigray · Simien

**Difficulty:** Easy–moderate  
**Best time:** October–June  
**Price from:** USD 3,900 per person (based on 2 people sharing)

### Overview

The full northern circuit — all four major destinations plus the Tigray rock churches. Eleven days is the minimum to do this route justice without rushing. You leave understanding why Ethiopians call their country "the cradle of civilisation" with complete seriousness.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 — Arrival Addis Ababa**  
Arrival, transfer to hotel. Optional National Museum visit. Overnight Addis.

**Day 2 — Fly to Axum**  
Morning flight to Axum. Afternoon: Great Stelae field. The largest standing obelisk in the world at 24 metres. The underground royal tombs — open to visitors. The Church of St Mary of Zion: the most sacred site in Ethiopian Orthodoxy, housing the alleged Ark of the Covenant (the Ark itself not visible). Evening walk in the small town. Overnight Axum.

**Day 3 — Axum continued, fly to Lalibela**  
Morning: Queen of Sheba's bath. The archaeological museum — extraordinary collection of Axumite coins, inscriptions, and artefacts. Afternoon flight to Lalibela. Overnight Lalibela.

**Day 4–5 — Lalibela churches**  
Two full days in Lalibela following the programme described in the 8-day itinerary above. Additional time for the morning liturgy (begins 4am — extraordinary), village walks, and the weekly market.

**Day 6 — Lalibela to Tigray (fly via Axum)**  
Flight north to Tigray region. Arrival at Gheralta. Afternoon: introduction to the rock church landscape — visit Abuna Yemata Guh, the cliff-face church accessible by a 45-minute climb. The views from the cliff are as extraordinary as the church itself. Overnight Gheralta area lodge.

**Day 7 — Tigray rock churches (full day)**  
Full day exploring the Gheralta church cluster — Debre Tsion, Maryam Korkor, Daniel Korkor. These churches require short hikes across spectacular rocky terrain. The interiors have remarkable 15th-century frescoes. Afternoon: Yeha temple — the oldest standing structure in Ethiopia, a pre-Axumite temple from the 5th century BC. Overnight Tigray.

**Day 8 — Tigray to Gondar**  
Morning: optional additional church visit. Drive or fly to Gondar. Afternoon: Royal Enclosure, Debre Berhan Selassie church. Overnight Gondar.

**Day 9–10 — Simien Mountains**  
As described in 8-day itinerary above. Two nights allows a better Simien experience — the second morning in the park, the gelada herds are often larger and more relaxed.

**Day 11 — Fly Gondar to Addis, departure**  
Morning flight to Addis. Transfer to Bole International for departure. Day room available if needed.

# Colors of the Omo Valley
## 8 Days · Southern Ethiopia · Mursi · Hamer · Karo

**Difficulty:** Moderate (rough roads, heat)  
**Best time:** September–January  
**Price from:** USD 3,200 per person (based on 2 people sharing)

### Overview

Eight days in the Omo Valley — the most concentrated cultural experience available in Ethiopia. The Mursi with their clay lip plates. The Hamer with their elaborate hairstyles and cattle ceremonies. The Karo with their extraordinary body painting and cliff paintings above the Omo River.

This is not comfortable travel. The roads are rough, the heat is significant, and the experiences are confronting. It is also one of the most powerful journeys available anywhere.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 — Addis to Arba Minch (fly)**  
Morning flight to Arba Minch. Afternoon: Lake Chamo boat trip — massive Nile crocodiles on the sandbanks, hippos, pelicans. The "crocodile market" — a sandbar where hundreds of crocs bask — is extraordinary. Overnight Arba Minch.

**Day 2 — Drive to Jinka**  
Full day drive south into the Omo Valley. Road passes through Konso territory — visit the UNESCO-listed Konso villages with their generation poles. Arrival Jinka. Overnight Jinka.

**Day 3 — Mursi village visit**  
Morning visit to Mursi territory. The Mursi are one of the most photographed people in the world, and one of the most misunderstood. Your guide will provide context that television documentaries consistently miss. Afternoon: Mago National Park entrance. Overnight Jinka.

**Day 4 — Key Afer market + Banna people**  
Thursday brings Key Afer market — the most extraordinary weekly gathering of Omo Valley tribes. Banna, Tsemay, Ari, and Hamer people all attend. Photography opportunities are extraordinary. Afternoon drive to Turmi. Overnight Turmi.

**Day 5 — Hamer village + bull jumping (if season)**  
Morning village visit to Hamer settlement. The Hamer are known for their elaborate ochre-dyed hairstyles, metal jewellery, and leather clothing. If timing is right (October–November peak), participation in Ukuli (bull jumping) ceremony — a young man runs across the backs of a line of cattle as his rite of passage. This is not staged; it is a real ceremony. Overnight Turmi.

**Day 6 — Karo people + Omo River**  
Drive to the Omo River to visit the Karo people — famous for elaborate body painting (white chalk, black charcoal, yellow ochre). Their cliff paintings above the river are the oldest in the Omo Valley. Afternoon canoe on the Omo River. Overnight Turmi area.

**Day 7 — Drive to Arba Minch**  
Long drive north via Konso. Overnight Arba Minch.

**Day 8 — Fly to Addis, departure**  
Morning flight to Addis Ababa. Transfer to Bole International for departure.

# Timkat Festival Tour
## 8 Days · Ethiopian Epiphany · January · Lalibela + Gondar

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Best time:** January 17–22 (fixed annually around January 19th)  
**Price from:** USD 3,800 per person (based on 2 people sharing)  
⚠️ **Book 9–12 months in advance**

### Overview

Timkat — Ethiopian Epiphany — is the most spectacular festival in the Ethiopian Orthodox calendar. It commemorates the baptism of Jesus in the Jordan River. On the eve of Timkat, the tabot (replica of the Ark of the Covenant) is carried from each church in a candlelit procession to a body of water, where it remains overnight. The following morning, priests bless the water and believers renew their baptism vows.

In Lalibela, this takes place in the rock-hewn church courtyards and along the processional routes through the town. In Gondar, the ceremony centres on Fasilides Bath, which is flooded for the occasion. Both are extraordinary. This itinerary includes both.

### Itinerary

**Day 1–2 — Arrive Addis, fly to Gondar**  
Pre-festival preparations visible throughout the city. Afternoon Royal Enclosure visit.

**Day 3 — Timkat in Gondar**  
The tabot processions begin the afternoon before Timkat. The Fasilides Bath flooded, filled with thousands of white-robed worshippers at dawn. Priests in gold vestments. Drums and chanting. Deacons dancing with prayer sticks and sistrums. This is one of the great spectacles of Ethiopia.

**Day 4 — Fly Gondar to Lalibela**  
Arrival in time for the beginning of Lalibela's Timkat preparations.

**Days 5–6 — Timkat in Lalibela**  
Lalibela's Timkat is experienced at a more intimate scale than Gondar's — the rock-hewn courtyard setting is more dramatic. The procession descends to Yemrehana Kristos church and the Beta Dawit area. The lighting of candles before sunrise, the chanting of the priests, the white-robed pilgrims filling the ancient courtyards — this is the experience that makes visitors describe Ethiopia as unlike anywhere else.

**Day 7 — Lalibela church exploration**  
Post-festival, the churches are quieter. Time to visit properly.

**Day 8 — Fly to Addis, departure**

# In Search of the Ethiopian Wolf
## 10 Days · Bale Mountains · Web Valley · Sanetti Plateau

**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Best time:** October–March  
**Price from:** USD 3,600 per person (based on 2 people sharing)

### Overview

The Ethiopian wolf is the world's rarest canid. Fewer than 500 remain. The Bale Mountains hold approximately 300 of them — the largest single population — in the Web Valley and on the Sanetti Plateau. This journey is designed around finding them, with time enough to observe behaviour rather than simply tick the species.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 — Addis Ababa arrival**  
Transfer to hotel. Optional National Museum visit.

**Day 2 — Drive to Bale Mountains (via Rift Valley lakes)**  
Full day drive south through the Rift Valley. Lake Langano for lunch and birdwatching — fish eagles, pelicans, flamingos. Continue to Bale Mountains. Overnight Robe or Dinsho area.

**Day 3 — Dinsho — mountain nyala and forest walks**  
Morning walk from Dinsho headquarters — mountain nyala habituated and often visible at close range. Warthog, Menelik's bushbuck, Abyssinian ground hornbill. Afternoon acclimatisation. Overnight Dinsho area.

**Day 4 — Web Valley — Ethiopian wolf (morning)**  
Early start (6am) for Web Valley — the best wolf-viewing location in the world. Your guide knows individual animals. At this hour, wolves are actively hunting giant mole rats on the valley floor. Remarkable predator-prey interaction. Afternoon: highland birding — Abyssinian longclaw, Blue-winged goose, Spot-breasted lapwing. Overnight Bale Mountain Lodge.

**Day 5 — Sanetti Plateau**  
Full day on the Sanetti Plateau at 4,000m — an Afroalpine landscape unlike anything else in Africa. Giant lobelia plants reaching 5 metres. Rock hyrax. More wolf sightings possible. The plateau edge looking north is one of the great views in Ethiopia. Overnight Bale Mountain Lodge.

**Day 6 — Web Valley return + Harenna Forest**  
Morning: final wolf viewing in Web Valley (most guests see wolves again). Afternoon descent into Harenna Forest — dense cloud forest with colobus monkeys, and extraordinary birdlife including Mountain Buzzard and Abyssinian Owl.

**Day 7–8 — Rift Valley lakes + birding**  
Return north via Lake Awasa, Lake Ziway, Lake Langano. Two days of serious lake birding — Africa's great white pelicans, yellow-billed stork, African spoonbill, Goliath heron, Lesser flamingo.

**Day 9 — Return to Addis Ababa**  
Return drive or fly. Overnight Addis Ababa.

**Day 10 — Departure**

# Danakil Depression & Erta Ale Lava Lake
## 7 Days · Afar Region · The World's Most Extreme Landscape

**Difficulty:** Strenuous (extreme heat, rough terrain, altitude change)  
**Best time:** November–February ONLY  
**Price from:** USD 3,400 per person (based on 2 people sharing)  
⚠️ Medical fitness required. Consult your doctor before booking.

### Overview

The Danakil Depression is one of the most extraordinary places on earth — and one of the most challenging. You will drive across a salt desert to the base of Erta Ale volcano, trek 2–3 hours across cooled lava under the night sky, and look into an active lava lake 30 metres below. The next day you will visit Dallol — the most colourful landscape on earth, sulphur springs creating towers of acid-yellow, green, and white mineral formations.

This is not comfortable. Temperatures at the base camp can reach 45°C even in December. The terrain is rough and the accommodation is basic. But nothing else looks like this.

### Itinerary

**Day 1 — Addis Ababa arrival**  
Transfer, hotel, briefing. Rest before tomorrow's early start.

**Day 2 — Fly to Mekele + drive to Erta Ale base camp**  
Morning flight to Mekele (Tigray). Meet 4x4 convoy. Drive south into the Afar Depression — watching the landscape change from highland to desert over 5 hours. Arrive base camp at the foot of Erta Ale. Basic camp dinner. Overnight at base camp (open-air or simple shelter).

**Day 3 — Erta Ale lava lake (night ascent)**  
Rest through the hottest part of the day. At approximately 9pm, begin the 2–3 hour trek to the crater rim by torchlight. Arrive the crater. Look into the lava lake — liquid rock boiling, orange and red in the darkness. This is one of only five permanent lava lakes in the world. Return to base camp before dawn. Overnight base camp.

**Day 4 — Drive to Dallol**  
Long drive across the salt flats. Pass salt-caravan camels — Afar nomads cutting and loading salt slabs by hand, exactly as they have for centuries. Arrive Dallol. The sulphur springs create formations unlike anything on earth — acid pools in bright green, yellow towers, crystalline white salt. Photography is extraordinary. Evening at Hamed Ela camp. Overnight Hamed Ela.

**Day 5 — Dallol continued + drive to Mekele**  
Morning: additional time at Dallol salt formations. Drive back north to Mekele. Overnight Mekele (proper hotel, first proper shower since Day 1).

**Day 6 — Mekele to Tigray rock churches**  
Afternoon visit to Tigray cliff churches as a recovery day. Gheralta cluster. Overnight Tigray.

**Day 7 — Fly Tigray to Addis, departure**

# Geological Wonders of Ethiopia
## 14 Days · Danakil · Bale · Rift Valley · Volcanic Heritage

**Difficulty:** Moderate–strenuous  
**Best time:** November–February  
**Price from:** USD 5,800 per person

### Overview

Ethiopia sits at one of the most geologically active points on earth — the junction of three tectonic plates, the formation of a new ocean, active volcanoes, and the world's most dramatic rift valley. This journey is designed for those interested in geology, plate tectonics, and the earth's most dynamic surface processes.

### Destinations

Danakil Depression (Erta Ale lava lake, salt flats, Dallol acid springs) · Afar Triangle (tectonic spreading zone) · Lake Awasa and the Rift Valley lakes · Bale Mountains (Afroalpine landscape formed by volcanic uplift) · Blue Nile gorge · Addis Ababa geological museum

# Spice & Fire — Ethiopian Culinary Journey
## 8 Days · Food · Coffee · Tej · Market Culture

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Best time:** Year-round  
**Price from:** USD 2,900 per person

### Overview

Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee, the origin of teff grain, and home to one of the world's most distinctive and complex cuisines. This journey is built around eating, drinking, and understanding Ethiopian food culture — from the coffee ceremony in a highland village, to the spice markets of Addis, to the tej houses of the north, to the injera-making tradition in a Lalibela home.

### Day-by-Day Summary

Day 1: Addis Ababa — specialty coffee tour, spice market, Ethiopian cooking class  
Day 2: Addis — Mercato, injera factory visit, evening tej house  
Day 3: Fly to Lalibela — traditional highland lunch, tella (local beer) brewery  
Day 4: Lalibela market and food culture  
Day 5: Fly to Gondar — royal banquet reconstruction dinner  
Day 6: Gondar — tej production, honey collection with local beekeepers  
Day 7: Fly to Kaffa region — wild coffee forest tour, coffee origin experience  
Day 8: Return Addis, departure

# Sawla Tours / Film-Production Brand Separation — V14.1

## Final decision

Sawla Tours is presented exclusively as an Ethiopia travel company. The Sawla Tours website does not market, describe, list or link to a film-production brand in traveler-facing content, navigation, footer, sitemap, structured data, AI discovery files or canonical journey pages.

## Technical migration rule

Two legacy Sawla Tours URLs that previously represented film-production content remain as permanent server-side redirects to `https://www.ethiopiafilmfixer.com/`:

- `/sawla-films`
- `/ethiopian-tour-themes/filming-ethiopia-fixer-production-logistics`

These redirects are a migration and user-recovery mechanism, not a hidden backlink or a Sawla Tours selling point. They preserve old bookmarks and external references while keeping both brands topically separate.

## SEO rule

Do not add hidden links, zero-size links, off-screen links, keyword-stuffed anchors, reciprocal sitewide links or `sameAs` assertions between the two domains. Google treats links created primarily to manipulate rankings as link spam. If a future cross-brand link becomes genuinely useful to users, it must be visible, contextual and editorially justified.

## Structured-data boundary

Sawla Tours schema must not use `sameAs`, `department`, `subOrganization`, `parentOrganization`, `hasPart` or similar properties to imply an unverified entity relationship with a production company.

## Future content rule

Do not reintroduce “in-house documentary division,” “only Ethiopian operator with a film division,” production credentials, film-fixing services or professional production calls to action into Sawla Tours content. Traveler photography remains a Sawla Tours product; commissioned media production does not.

# Sawla Tours V17 — Claims and Evidence Register

**Review checkpoint:** 14 June 2026

## Publication rule

A stable fact may be evergreen. A changing fact must carry a current source or a visible review rule. A departure-specific promise belongs in the proposal or trip dossier, not in permanent marketing copy.

## Stable editorial claims

These can remain with periodic review:

- Sawla Tours is based in Addis Ababa.
- Journeys are private and tailor-made.
- Ethiopia contains major highland, Rift Valley, desert, forest and cultural landscapes.
- Lalibela, Aksum, Gondar, Harar, Konso, Simien and Bale have internationally recognized heritage or conservation significance where accurately described.

## Date-sensitive claims

These require live confirmation before publication and before departure:

- visa eligibility, entry points, documents, fees and processing guidance;
- government travel advice and no-travel boundaries;
- flight routes, operating days, connections, baggage and fares;
- park, church, museum and monastery access;
- regional transport and road conditions;
- market and ceremony dates;
- public-health entry requirements and recommendations;
- accommodation opening and standards.

## Departure-specific claims

These should appear only after confirmation in a proposal or dossier:

- exact vehicle model, seating, charging and air conditioning;
- scout, ranger, escort or authority requirements;
- mobile-camp tent, bathroom, power, sanitation and catering specification;
- guide identity, language and credentials;
- domestic-flight numbers and timings;
- community or ceremony access;
- volcanic viewing and crater approach;
- wildlife targets and recent locations.

## Evidence required before adding

Do not publish until records are supplied and reviewed:

- licence number, issuer, scope and validity;
- insurance provider, policy scope and validity;
- professional memberships;
- awards;
- guide certifications;
- named staff biographies and experience figures;
- aggregate review scores or percentages;
- total traveler/journey counts.

## Current source hierarchy

1. Ethiopian government/authority sources for entry and regulatory matters.
2. The traveler’s own government advice for travel-advisory and insurance implications.
3. WHO or qualified travel-health professionals for health guidance.
4. Park, heritage, religious and regional authorities for access.
5. Confirmed supplier and field information for current operations.
6. Guidebooks only for historical and background context, not live logistics.

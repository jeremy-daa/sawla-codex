**Date:** 29 May 2026
**Purpose:** Step-by-step guide for organizing, naming, resizing, and uploading photos
**Total photos needed:** 350–500 across the full website

---

## STEP 1 — SET UP YOUR LAPTOP FOLDER STRUCTURE

Create one master folder on your Desktop. Name it exactly:

```
sawla-photos/
  01-originals/        ← All original files — never edit or delete these
  02-renamed/          ← Files after you apply the naming convention
  03-resized/          ← Files resized and ready to upload to Claude
  04-uploaded/         ← Move files here after Claude has reviewed them
  05-approved/         ← Final approved photos, ready for developer
```

**Why this structure:**
- Originals stay safe (never lose source files)
- You always know the status of each photo
- Developer gets only the approved, final files

## STEP 2 — NAMING CONVENTION

### Format
```
[section]-[subject]-[descriptor].jpg
```

### Rules
- Lowercase only (no capital letters)
- Hyphens between words (no spaces, no underscores)
- No special characters (no ?, &, %, #, etc.)
- Maximum 50 characters per filename
- Never use camera defaults (IMG_1234.jpg, DSC_0456.jpg, etc.)

## STEP 3 — RESIZE BEFORE UPLOADING

### Why You Must Resize

Original camera photos are typically 20–50 MB. These are too large to upload efficiently to Claude. Resize to the specifications below — the quality is identical for review purposes and for the website.

### Specifications by Use

| Photo Type | Width | Height | Approximate File Size |
|-----------|-------|--------|----------------------|
| Hero images (page headers) | 1920px | 1080px | Under 3 MB |
| Gallery images | 1200px | 800px | Under 2 MB |
| Team portraits / headshots | 800px | 800px | Under 1 MB |
| Wildlife photos | 1200px | 800px | Under 2 MB |
| Action / candid shots | 1200px | 800px | Under 2 MB |

**Maximum per photo when uploading to Claude: 5 MB**
**Format: JPG (quality 80–85%)**

---

### Free Resize Tools

**Option 1 — Squoosh (browser, no install)**
- Go to: https://squoosh.app
- Drag your photo in
- Set resize: 1920px width (or 800px for portraits)
- Format: MozJPEG, quality 80
- Download the compressed file

**Option 2 — Mac (built-in Preview)**
- Open photo in Preview
- Tools menu → Adjust Size
- Set width (e.g. 1920)
- File → Export → JPG, quality 80%

**Option 3 — Windows (Photos app)**
- Open in Photos
- Edit → Resize
- Select width
- Save a copy

**Option 4 — Adobe Lightroom (if you have it)**
- Export → Resize to fit: Long Edge 1920px
- Quality: 80%
- Format: JPG
- Color space: sRGB

## STEP 4 — HOW TO UPLOAD TO CLAUDE

### The Upload Button

In this chat interface (claude.ai), look for the paperclip icon or attachment button at the bottom of the message box. Click it to select photos from your computer. You can select multiple photos at once.

### Maximum Per Message

Upload **5–10 photos per message**. This is the sweet spot — enough for a useful batch review, not so many that details get missed.

### What to Say When You Upload

Always tell me:

1. **Which page/section:** "These are for the Lalibela destination page"
2. **What's in the batch:** "1 hero, 4 gallery shots, 1 portrait"
3. **The source:** "From Sawla Films extraction" / "From team photo shoot" / "From client photos"
4. **Any concerns:** "Not sure if the lighting is good enough" / "Which of these 3 hero options is strongest?"

### Example Upload Messages

**Good message:**
> "I'm uploading 7 photos for the Lalibela destination page. There are 2 hero candidates (wide church exterior), 3 gallery shots (church interiors and a priest), and 2 landscape shots. All from Sawla Films extraction. Which hero is strongest and are they all usable?"

**Another good message:**
> "These are 6 team headshots from our photo shoot yesterday — 3 travel specialists and 3 guides. Please review quality and tell me if any need to be retaken. Names: specialist-sarah-m, specialist-michael-k, specialist-anna-t, guide-abraham-t, guide-daniel-m, guide-sara-h."

**What I will return after each batch:**
- Quality assessment for each photo (usable / not usable / needs edit)
- Selection recommendation (which to use and where exactly)
- Alt text written for every approved photo
- Any retake recommendations

## STEP 6 — WHAT I CAN DO WITH YOUR PHOTOS

Once you upload photos, I can help with:

### Quality Review
- Is the photo sharp / in focus?
- Is the lighting good enough for web use?
- Is the composition strong (rule of thirds, horizon straight)?
- Does it feel authentic or staged?
- Is it on-brand for Sawla Tours?

### Selection Decisions
- If you upload 5 options for one position, I'll tell you which is strongest and why
- I'll flag any photos that need to be retaken
- I'll suggest better alternatives from your batch if one photo doesn't work

### Placement Decisions
- I'll tell you exactly which page section each photo belongs to
- Hero vs gallery vs sidebar decisions
- Cross-page reuse recommendations

### Alt Text (SEO)
For every approved photo, I'll write SEO-optimised alt text. Example:

| File | Alt Text |
|------|----------|
| `dest-lalibela-bete-medhane-alem.jpg` | "Bete Medhane Alem rock-hewn church, Lalibela Ethiopia — largest monolithic church in the world" |
| `species-ethiopian-wolf-portrait.jpg` | "Ethiopian wolf (Canis simensis) in Bale Mountains National Park, Ethiopia's rarest endemic predator" |
| `about-guide-abraham-t-lalibela.jpg` | "Abraham T., Sawla Tours licensed guide, Lalibela specialist with 12 years guiding experience" |

### Photo Inventory CSV
After reviewing each batch, I'll give you a running CSV entry to add to your master photo inventory spreadsheet.

---

## STEP 7 — WHAT I CANNOT DO

**I cannot:**
- Edit or retouch photos (I can review and advise, not edit)
- Access files on your laptop directly (you must upload them here)
- Extract stills from video files (do this in your video software first)
- Resize files for you (use Squoosh or Lightroom before uploading)

**For editing photos:** Use Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop, or GIMP (free)
**For extracting video stills:** Use DaVinci Resolve (free), Final Cut, or Premiere
